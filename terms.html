Important Golf Terms

These are the basic course, gameplay, and scoring terms you'll here on the course or when talking about golf. There are several more than what is listed here so don't be surprised if you here something else. Golf is meant to be fun so some of these terms may seem clever or even strange. If you hear someone say "handicap" they're referring to the average strokes a player hits over par. It'll sound like, "What's your handicap?" A player would respond with a number. Scratch golfers are able to play a course at even par on average.

Course

  • Fairway: a long stretch of neatly maintained grass between the tee box and green.
  • Rough: taller grass, sometimes much thicker, bordering the fairway and sometimes around the green.
  • Green: smooth grass area at the end of the fairway where the hole is.
  • Hazard: any area on the course that is problematic for you to shoot a good score. Avoid hazards (water hazard).
  • Bunker: a type of hazard filled with sand. Sometimes very deep. You can play your ball out of a bunker.
  • The Beach: a slang term for bunker. Think of sandy beach. You don't want to end up on the beach during your round.

Gameplay

  • Drive: used to describe your shot with the driver. "Nice drive."
  • Approach: used to describe a shot taken off the fairway on to the green.
  • Putt: used to talk about a shot taken with the putter on the green.
  • Away: refers to the ball that is furthest from the hole. "You're away."
  • The Turn: this is the halfway point of your round of golf.
  • Mulligan: a term that refers to a "do-over." Only used in casual, non-tournament play.

Scoring

  • Par: number of strokes a golfer is recommended to hit. This can be different on each hole. Par 3, Par 4, or Par 5.
  • Birdie: a score of 1 under par on a single hole.
  • Eagle: a score of 2 under par on a single hole.
  • Double Eagle: extremely rare. This is a score of 3 under par. AKA Albatross.
  • Bogey: a score of 1 over par. There are also double and triple bogey (2 and 3) over par.
  • Ace: hole in one! Usually only obtainable on Par 3's.
