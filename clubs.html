Clubs

Below you'll find a table with a breakdown of commonly used golf clubs. Take your time to familiarize yourself with each club. You'll notice the higher the number the shorter you'll hit the ball with it. The club consists of three components; the head, the shaft, and the grip. Keep in mind there are many different club set ups to maximize a players abilities based on their swing.

Woods

Woods are used to hit longer distance shots. These clubs have larger heads and longer shafts. The majority of these clubs are used for over 200 yards.

Irons

The irons are the most used clubs. The closer to the green, the higher the numbered iron you'll use. Most golfers carry a 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9 iron. The lower numbered irons are less forgiving and harder to hit because the loft is less. As a beginner, if you struggle hitting lower numbered irons you could consider replacing them with higher numbered woods or a hybrid (combo club with a wood head design and an iron length shaft).

Wedges

Wedges are similar to irons. These clubs are great for when a players distance to the green is less than 120 yards. Like the standard irons, most golfers carry multiple wedges with different lofts. Wedges are great for shots around the green that require touch and finesse.

**Yardage below is averaged. Depending on your swing you may hit the ball further or shorter.**

Club Type Loft Distance Description
Driver Wood 9° or 10.5° 250-300 yards This club is used on the tee box. Longest and furthest club. Sometimes called the 1 wood.
3 Wood Wood 15° 225-250 yards Used for long distance shots off the fairway. Similar to a driver but it has a smaller head and shorter shaft.
5 Wood Wood 18° 200-225 yards Used for long distance shots off the fairway. Similar to a driver but it has a smaller head and shorter shaft.
4 Iron 21° 210 yards The 4 iron and below are the hardest irons to hit. If you're struggling do not be afraid to replace this iron with a higher numbered fairway wood or a low numbered hybrid.
5 Iron 23.5° 190 yards The 5 iron is sometimes replaced with a hybrid or fairway wood. Many beginners steer clear of irons this low.
6 Iron 26.5° 175 yards Long distance iron shots tend to be more difficult. You'll have less room for error in your ball striking for this iron.
7 Iron 30.5° 165 yards The 7 iron is when beginner golfers may start to struggle with control of the ball. The lower loft of the face makes it less forgiving.
8 Iron 35° 150 yards The 8 iron is a mid range iron. Most 2nd or 3rd shots will be from around 150 yards.
9 Iron 40° 130 yards The 9 iron is the most forgiving iron. Great for shots into the green from less than 140 yards
PW Wedge 45° 120 yards A pitching wedge is the go to club for short full swing shots. Like other wedges, you can use this club for chipping as well.
AW Wedge 50° 110 yards Many players will use the Approach Wedge for short pitch shots. This club is also called a gap wedge.
SW Wedge 52° Less than 100 yards SW stands for sand wedge. This club is mostly used for sand shots around the green but can be used for chipping as well.
Putter Putter N/A N/A Your putter is used when you're on the green. The face is flat and the club head holds the weight. There are several styles of putters and if you enjoy playing I suggest doing your own research to find which is best for you.
