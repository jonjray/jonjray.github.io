Etiquette

Before hitting the course for your first round of golf I'd suggest learning proper golf etiquette. It's a good idea to have this knowledge before beginning your golf adventure.

Begin Here

  • Arrive early. This will give you time to warm up and be ready. Don't want to hurt yourself on the first hole!
  • Practice with each club. Start with your AW or PW and work your way up towards the driver.
  • After initial warm up, practice chipping. There's usually a designated area for chipping.
  • Lastly, practice your putting. Use the same balls in putting practice that you'll use on the course.
  • Don't overdue the warmup. No need to hit hundreds of balls on the range.
  • Familiarize yourself with course rules and the scorecard.
  • Avoid profanity when grouped with players you don't know.

During Play

  • Silence! When other players in your group are hitting it is common courtesy to be quiet.
  • Avoid slow play. When it's your turn be ready. Once you address the ball it shouldn't take more than 30 to 45 sec to hit.
  • Replace divots. Simple, if you take a chunk of grass out when you swing get it and put it back.
  • Rake the bunker after your shot.
  • Repair your ball mark on the green. It's difficult to putt if the green is bumpy from ball marks. (ball mark is a small indent from your ball impacting the green)
  • Mark your ball with a coin or ball marker while other players are putting.
  • Don't walk through fellow players putting lines (the line the player plans the putt their ball on to the hole)

Miscellaneous

  • HAVE FUN!!
  • Congratualte players for nice shots.
  • If you're unsure about something just ask. Most players are polite and are out to have fun. I've never met an unhelpful player.
  • Yell "FORE" immediately if your ball has a chance of hitting someone.
  • Be Polite. Act as if you're playing with your grandparents at first. This will protect you from offending someone who may be strict on USGA rules.
  • At the end of the round shake hands with your fellow players. Thank them for the round.
